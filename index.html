Concept

Understanding disruption before it becomes obvious.

Disruptive Foresight is a concept-led learning platform for reading technological change as it unfolds. It helps students connect signals, business models, institutions, and strategic response in one structured environment.

Created by Valery Chistov, Assistant Professor at Deusto Business School, the platform turns course material into a coherent editorial space: one where digital transformation, digital finance, and long-range strategic thinking can be studied with depth rather than as disconnected topics.

Definition

What Disruptive Foresight means in practice.

Disruptive Foresight combines strategic foresight with disruptive innovation. It gives students a way to interpret how technologies, institutions, and business models evolve together, and why that co-evolution matters for managers, policymakers, and financial actors.

Rather than isolating one trend at a time, the platform teaches students to read systems: how digital infrastructures shape behavior, how new entrants reframe market logic, and how incumbent organizations respond when established assumptions no longer hold.

Platform

An editorial interface for structured learning.

Each course works like an online book. Chapters, subsection navigation, and starter resources are organized into a calm, readable interface so the focus stays on the argument rather than the mechanics of finding content.

The platform is meant to be used before class, during seminars, and when students are developing their own analytical position on technology-driven change.

Platform Feature
